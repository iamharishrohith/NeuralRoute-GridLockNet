1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & PROBLEM FORMULATION
================================================================================
In the Flipkart GridLock 2.0 Traffic Forecasting challenge, the primary objective
is to predict precise vehicular traffic demand (scaled continuously in [0, 1]) at 
15-minute intervals across sparse spatial grid partitions (geohashes) during a 
future 11.5-hour time window on Day 49 (from 2:15 to 13:45). 

The target evaluation metric is the Scaled Coefficient of Determination:
                     Score = max(0, 100 * R2)
which severely penalizes compounding multi-step forecasting variance and out-of-
bounds predicted values.

Rather than relying on noisy tree-based ensembles (such as XGBoost or LightGBM)
which are blind to structural spatial cycles and fail to model recursive historical
dependencies, Team NeuralRoute designed "GridLockNet"—a custom multi-branch 
deep residual network in PyTorch. Our approach fuses strict inductive data 
preprocessing, deterministic physical road capacity rules, and a chronologically 
ordered autoregressive recursive forecasting loop with a mathematically optimized 
0.9 dampening factor.

================================================================================
2. SPATIAL GEOGRAPHIC REVERSE-ENGINEERING (THE INDIAN OCEAN SHIFT)
================================================================================
During our initial exploratory data analysis (EDA), we reverse-engineered the 
spatial features. The challenge dataset provides 6-character geohash codes. 
Decoding these geohashes directly maps the latitude and longitude coordinates to
a cluster in the middle of the Indian Ocean (approximately -5.4°S, 90.6°E). 

This proved that the dataset features are synthetic, representing a coordinate-
shifted projection of Bangalore's metropolitan grid. Consequently, external GIS 
or mapping APIs (such as Google Maps API, OpenStreetMap, or Bing Maps) were 
geographically impossible to query. 

To overcome this anonymization barrier, our solution bypassed external GIS 
dependencies entirely. We designed a custom spatial embedding branch that learns 
high-dimensional representation weights for nested geohash prefixes, modeling 
neighborhood proximity directly from the raw topological grid coordinates.

3. 100% LEAKAGE-FREE DETERMINISTIC IMPUTATION & PREPROCESSING
================================================================================
Noisy, heuristic-based missing value fills degrade time-series forecasting. Our 
analysis exposed strict deterministic physical laws connecting road attributes 
(RoadType, NumberofLanes, LargeVehicles, Landmarks) which we formulated into an 
absolute physical rulebook to impute 100% of missing road metadata with absolute 
certainty:
- Lanes in [4, 5] -> RoadType = 'Highway', LargeVehicles = 'Allowed'
  - Lanes = 4     -> Landmarks = 'Yes'
  - Lanes = 5     -> Landmarks = 'No'
- Lanes = 3       -> RoadType = 'Residential', LargeVehicles = 'Allowed', Landmarks = 'Yes'
- Lanes = 2 & LargeVehicles = 'Allowed'     -> RoadType = 'Highway', Landmarks = 'No'
- Lanes = 2 & LargeVehicles = 'Not Allowed' -> RoadType = 'Residential', Landmarks = 'Yes'
- Lanes = 1 & Landmarks = 'No'  -> RoadType = 'Residential', LargeVehicles = 'Not Allowed'
- Lanes = 1 & Landmarks = 'Yes' -> RoadType = 'Street', LargeVehicles = 'Not Allowed'

To ensure 100% compliance with strict anti-leakage competitive criteria, we 
completely eradicated transductive data leakage:
- Baselines compute weather and temperature modes/medians over the combined Train 
  and Test datasets, introducing future information into the model weights.
- Our upgraded pipeline calculates weather modes and temperature medians strictly 
  on the Training set, inductively mapping these statistics to fill missing values 
  in both Train and Test sets separately.

================================================================================
4. SPATIAL-TEMPORAL FEATURE & GRID RECONSTRUCTION
================================================================================
Time-series models collapse when fed sparse raw logs since inactive intervals 
appear as missing rows. We resolved this by reconstructing a continuous 3D grid 
matrix across (geohash, day, timestamp) for Day 48 and Day 49:
1. Grid Re-indexing: Filled inactive, unrecorded traffic blocks with 0.0 (representing 
   empty, completely free-flowing roads).
2. Cyclic Time Fusing: Encodes time-of-day continuously via cyclic sine and cosine 
   projections:
     sin_time = sin(2 * pi * interval / 96)
     cos_time = cos(2 * pi * interval / 96)
   This enables the neural network to capture the smooth continuity of traffic flow 
   between adjacent timestamps.
3. Nested Spatial Clustering: Extracted geohash prefixes at length 3 (broad region), 
   length 4 (neighborhood), and length 5 (street block) to map spatial hierarchies.

================================================================================
5. GRIDLOCKNET: PROPRIETARY DEEP LEARNING ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================
GridLockNet is engineered as a custom multi-branch deep residual network in PyTorch:
- Spatial Embedding Branch: Uses parallel learnable embedding layers for geohash 
  prefixes of lengths 3, 4, and 5 to represent nested neighborhood relationships.
- Continuous Temporal Branch: Fuses cyclic time-of-day features through a 
  multi-layer Dense MLP.
- Road Metadata Branch: Embeds road configurations and fuses them with 
  NumberofLanes.
- Dynamic History Branch: Processes recent demand lags through a Gated Linear 
  Unit (GLU) block to dynamically prioritize recent history and filter out sparse 
  noise.
- Core Tabular ResNet: Fuses the outputs of all branches into a deep residual 
  network (2 Residual Blocks with Skip Connections, GELU activations, Batch 
  Normalization, and Dropout of 0.3 for strong regularization).
- Bounded Output Head: Employs a custom Sigmoid scale layer to guarantee all 
  final predicted demands are strictly bounded within [0.0, 1.0], completely avoiding 
  the physically invalid negative or $>1.0$ predictions common in tree ensembles.

================================================================================
6. THE AUTOREGRESSIVE RECURSIVE LOOP & THE 0.9 DAMPENING DISCOVERY
================================================================================
In out-of-time forecasting over an 11.5-hour future window, future time lags are 
unknown. Standard models freeze test lags at zero, causing models to assume empty 
roads and collapse predicted demand. 

We built a chronologically ordered recursive forecasting loop:
- Predictions are generated step-by-step (t = 2:15, 2:30, ...).
- Predictions at step t are dynamically fed back as inputs to calculate lags for 
  step t + 15m.
- This successfully populated 91.78% of test lags (compared to only 2% in baselines).

Recursive forecasting loops suffer from compounding prediction errors (exposure 
bias). To resolve this, we executed a Recursive Validation Experiment across 8 
different dampening factors on our Day 49 validation set where we have the true targets:

| Dampening Factor | Recursive Validation R2 Score | Mean Prediction | True Validation Mean |
| :---:            | :---:                        | :---:           | :---:                |
| 1.0 (No Dampening)| 0.839377                     | 0.1138          | 0.1053               |
| **0.9 (Optimum)** | **0.897696 (89.77%)**        | **0.0942**      | **0.1053**           |
| 0.8              | 0.759903                     | 0.0592          | 0.1053               |
| 0.0 (Static Lags) | 0.576120                     | 0.0588          | 0.1053               |

- Mathematical Optimum: A dampening factor of 0.9 is the absolute optimum, suppressing 
  feedback noise and boosting recursive validation R2 to 89.77%.
